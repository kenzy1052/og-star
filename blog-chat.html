Top 10 Hidden Gems in Ghana You Should Visit in 2025

Beyond the coastal castles and Accra's pulse, Ghana hides intimate landscapes, sacred villages, and forests waiting to be explored. Here are ten places that reveal a gentler, more unexpected Ghana.

From misted waterfalls to stilt villages floating on expansive lakes, Ghana’s diversity rewards travelers who wander off the classic routes. These destinations combine culture, wildlife, and dramatic scenery — perfect for explorers seeking authenticity.

Below we highlight five standout sites with quick practical tips and links so you can learn more before you go.

1. Wli Waterfalls — Volta Region

Wli is West Africa’s tallest waterfall. The walk through the Agumatsa forest is filled with birds and butterflies; during the wet season, the falls thunder with extra force. Practical tip: hire a local guide in Hohoe to learn about the surrounding wildlife and community conservation efforts. Learn more: Wli Waterfalls (Wikipedia).

2. Nzulezo Stilt Village — Western Region

Accessed only by canoe, Nzulezo is an entire community built above the waters of Lake Tadane. The journey through mangroves is as memorable as the village itself. Plan for a guided canoe trip from Beyin or Elmina. More: Nzulezo Stilt Village.

3. Shai Hills Resource Reserve — Greater Accra

Just an hour from Accra, Shai Hills blends hiking, dramatic rock outcrops, and resident wildlife. Bring sturdy shoes for scrambling and early-morning visits to avoid midday heat. Read: Shai Hills Resource Reserve.

4. Tafi Atome Monkey Sanctuary — Volta

A community-managed sanctuary that protects Mona monkeys while offering visitors close encounters and cultural insight. Combine with a visit to nearby craft markets for an enriching day-trip. Info: Tafi Atome Monkey Sanctuary.

5. Larabanga Mosque & Northern Heritage

Larabanga’s 15th-century mud mosque is one of West Africa’s oldest Islamic structures and a striking stop when visiting Mole National Park for safaris and birdwatching. Practical link: Larabanga Mosque. For safaris, see Mole National Park.

More spots & how to plan

Other treasures include Boabeng-Fiema’s sacred monkey forest and lesser-known waterfalls and coastal fishing villages. For logistics, consider hiring local drivers or reputable guides who know seasonal conditions; many remote spots require a sturdy vehicle and flexibility.

Quick travel tips: Respect community customs, carry cash for remote areas, and opt for locally-run lodges where your spending supports the local economy.

How to Travel on a Budget and Still Live Your Dream Vacation

Budget travel today is about smart choices, not sacrifice. With planning and a few strategic habits you can enjoy rich experiences while keeping costs low.

Start with flexible dates and flying smart: small shifts in departure or arrival days can save hundreds. Use search aggregators and set alerts, and be open to alternate airports and overnight transfers.

Combine platform tools with local knowledge to find affordable, authentic stays and meals.

Book smarter — flights & transport

Use tools like Skyscanner and Google Flights to compare prices and set alerts. Consider overnight buses or trains for long distances — they save on accommodation and are often comfortable.

Accommodation that feels local

Look beyond chain hotels. Platforms like Airbnb, guesthouses, and small inns offer authentic experiences. House-sitting networks such as TrustedHousesitters provide free stays in exchange for caretaking tasks.

Eat like a local

Street food and market stalls often give the best food for the best prices. Seek lunchtime specials and local bakeries for inexpensive, delicious meals.

Free & low-cost experiences

Find free walking tours, parks, city museums with free days, and scenic hiking routes. Many festivals host free performances — plan your trip to coincide with local events to maximize value.

Timing is everything

Travel in shoulder seasons to avoid crowds and high prices. For example, late spring or early autumn often delivers mild weather and lower rates for many destinations.

Final tip: Keep an “experience fund” for one special splurge per trip — you’ll remember it longer than a string of small frugal choices.

Landing Your Dream Job in Dubai: A Step-by-Step Guide for 2025

Dubai offers tax-free salaries and global mobility, but its job market has local rhythms. This guide helps you approach the search with confidence and cultural insight.

Target industries: tech, finance, hospitality, healthcare, logistics, and creative media. Free zones such as Dubai Internet City and Dubai Media City host many international firms — identify which free zones align with your skills.

Start with a polished CV and a LinkedIn presence tailored to the Middle East market.

Where to search

Use region-focused job boards like Bayt, GulfTalent, and specialized recruiters. LinkedIn is essential — follow company pages and recruiters active in Dubai.

CV & documents

Dubai CVs often include a short professional photo and a clear salary expectation. Make sure degrees and certificates are attested per UAE requirements if a hire progresses to visa stage.

Visa & sponsorship

Most employment visas are employer-sponsored. There are also Golden Visa programs for exceptional talent and remote-work visas that allow living in Dubai while employed elsewhere — research which fits your situation.

Negotiation & packages

Beyond base salary, negotiate housing allowance, flight allowance, medical coverage, and education support (if relevant). Ask about end-of-service benefits and probation terms.

Cultural fit & interview tips

Understand local business etiquette, show cultural respect, and emphasize team-orientation. Prepare examples of cross-cultural collaboration and adaptability — these qualities are highly valued.

Resources: LinkedIn, Glassdoor for salary benchmarks, and regional job boards like GulfTalent.

Life in Dubai: How to Work, Stay, and Thrive Abroad

Moving to Dubai is exhilarating and practical. This piece covers neighborhoods, cost of living realities, healthcare, schooling, cultural norms, and strategies for long-term success.

Where you live matters — Dubai Marina and Downtown suit urban professionals; Arabian Ranches and Meadows appeal to families. Factor commuting time and lifestyle when choosing an area.

Housing often dominates budgets, so explore allowances and negotiate as part of your package.

Neighborhoods & commuting

Marina and Downtown are vibrant but pricier. For more affordable options, consider Discovery Gardens or Deira with easy metro access. Driving is common but traffic can be heavy; proximity reduces stress and time lost commuting.

Cost of living & compensation

While salaries are tax-free, high rents can offset gains. Always compare packages including housing allowance, flights, and health insurance. Use resources like Numbeo and Glassdoor for up-to-date benchmarks.

Healthcare, schools & daily life

Healthcare is excellent but insurance-based — confirm coverage levels. International schools are diverse but expensive; factor education into family relocation plans. Budget for weekends, travel, and leisure — Dubai’s attractions and regional flights make exploration easy.

Culture & belonging

Respect local norms: modest dress in public spaces outside beaches, awareness around Ramadan, and photographed consent. Build community through expat networks, sports clubs, and local volunteer groups to make Dubai feel like home.

Long-term strategy

Stability in Dubai is about finances, legal clarity, and professional growth. Keep skills current, build a local network, and maintain ties to your home country for future flexibility.

Suggested community resources: Meetup, InterNations, and local Facebook groups for housing and jobs.
